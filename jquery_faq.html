Here are some Frequently Asked Questions about National Parks...

1. Who is the Director of the National Park Service?
Dan (the Man) Smith, of course.
2. What are concessions?
Concessions -
a. a thing that is granted, especially in response to demands; a thing conceded.
b. a preferential rate or allowance given by an organization.
3. Can I bring a pet to a National Park?
You can to some. Not to some others though.
4. What do I need to know about driving off-road in a National Park?
Lots of things. Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal, bruh.

Here are some fun facts about National Parks...


Redwood National and State Park - CA

  • Redwoods are the tallest trees on earth
  • Hikes in Redwood Nat'l Park can last anywhere from one day to one week
  • Miles and Miles of bike trails are available for use
  • Guests can view wildlife ranging from gray whales and seals, to elk and meadowlarks

Big Bend National Park - TX

  • Activities include: day hikes, stargazing, fishing, and horseback riding
  • Black bears are native to the area
  • Various lodgings, tent camping, and RV campsites are available
  • Big Bend is the 15th largest National Park in the United States

Joshua Tree National Park - CA

  • Combines the ecosystems of the Mojave and the Colorado combine in Joshua Tree
  • Pets of any kind are not allowed on hiking trails
  • Camping, hiking, and Ranger-led programs are available
  • The Joshua Tree is like, totally U2's best album. I mean, the guitar intro to "Where the Streets Have No Name" alone... Geez man, it just kills me
